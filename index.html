about me


My name is Saul Hudson, and I’m from Los-Angeles, California.
I’m a musician and a psalmist, but I’m mostly known for my guitar skills and the band I’m a part of – "Guns N’ Roses".
If you don’t know until now who I am, you will probably recognize by my nickname “Slash”.

So here what there is to know about me.
I born in Hampstead, England and right now I’m 54 years old. Since the age 15, when my grandmother gave my first guitar, me and the guitar was always a
successful love story and most of the time you’ll probably find me with a guitar in my hands.

After I few years when I got my first guitar and made improvements with my guitar skills, I had a dream to join a band and become famous!
At first, it wasn’t easy at all, I formed a few bands with a childhood friend but those bands have not been successful and not recognized.
In the end, after a few failing attempts, I joined the band “Guns N’ Roses” and together we began to conquer and music world, and especially the hard-rock genre!

For me, the thing I’m mostly happy about is that the in songs we made, my guitar playing made a lot of inspiration in fans around the world, and it inspired them to study
how to play a guitar and to dare making their dreams come true!

In time, I got some nice rewards for my skills. Here some of them:

  • In 2004, the magazine “Guitar World” placed my riff (opening guitar) in the song “Sweet Child o’Mine” at the 1st place on their list of “The 100 Greatest Riffs”.

  • In 2008, ‘Guitar World’ ranked my solo in the song “November Rain” at 6th on their list of “The 100 Greatest Guitar Solos”.

  • In 2009, Time magazine names me as one of “The 10 Best Electric Guitar Players”.

    search engines retrieve macro contents. Search is very fast but many times results are inaccurate or more than users can chew.

    search engines retrieve tags with micro contents. The process of tagging is manual, tedious and covers negligible percents of the WWW.

    search engines will retrieve micro content texts which were tagged automatically.This implies translating billions of Web 1.0 macro contents into micro contents. The result could be more precise search because tagging can solve part of the ambiguity that homonyms and synonyms introduce into the process of Search.

    Companies publish content that people consume (e.g. Ynet)

    People publish content that other people can consume, companies build platforms that let people publish content for other people (e.g. YouTube, Wikipedia)

    People build applications that people can interact with, companies build platforms that let people publish services by leveraging the associations between people or special content ( e. g. Facebook, Instagram)
